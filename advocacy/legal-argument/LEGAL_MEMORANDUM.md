## 3. LEGAL ANALYSIS: DOMESTIC CONSTITUTIONAL VIOLATIONS

### 3.1 Fourth Amendment Violations

> "The right of the people to be secure in their persons, houses, papers, and effects, against unreasonable searches and seizures, shall not be violated, and no Warrants shall issue, but upon probable cause..."
> — U.S. Constitution, Amendment IV

**Violation Category A: Warrantless Home Entries**

Federal agents conducted forced entries into private residences using only administrative warrants. An administrative warrant (Form I-200) is an internal agency document, **not** a judicial warrant issued by a neutral magistrate upon a finding of probable cause.

**Legal Standard:** *Payton v. New York*, 445 U.S. 573 (1980) — Absent exigent circumstances, law enforcement may not enter a home to make an arrest without a judicial warrant.

**Application:** The January 11, 2026 raid on the home of U.S. citizen ChongLy Scott Thao, conducted with a battering ram and without presentation of a judicial warrant, constitutes a presumptive Fourth Amendment violation.

**Violation Category B: Suspicionless Stops and Detentions**

Federal agents conducted traffic stops and street detentions without reasonable articulable suspicion.

**Legal Standard:** *Terry v. Ohio*, 392 U.S. 1 (1968) — An officer may conduct a brief investigatory stop only when the officer has reasonable, articulable suspicion that criminal activity is afoot.

**Application:** The federal court's injunction specifically noted that "following an agent's vehicle does not, by itself, create reasonable suspicion to justify a vehicle stop."

### 3.2 First Amendment Violations

> "Congress shall make no law... abridging the freedom of speech, or of the press; or the right of the people peaceably to assemble..."
> — U.S. Constitution, Amendment I

**Violation Category: Suppression of Peaceful Protest**

Federal agents deployed chemical agents (pepper balls, tear gas, flashbangs) against peaceful protesters and journalists. A federal judge issued an injunction barring these tactics.

**Legal Standard:** Protected speech and assembly may not be suppressed absent a compelling government interest pursued through narrowly tailored means. *Ward v. Rock Against Racism*, 491 U.S. 781 (1989).

**Retaliation Evidence:** The State of Minnesota's lawsuit alleges the entire "Operation Metro Surge" constitutes political retaliation against a state that did not support the President electorally, implicating viewpoint discrimination.

### 3.3 Tenth Amendment and Federalism Violations

> "The powers not delegated to the United States by the Constitution... are reserved to the States respectively, or to the people."
> — U.S. Constitution, Amendment X

**Anti-Commandeering Doctrine:** The federal government may not compel state or local governments to implement federal regulatory programs. *Printz v. United States*, 521 U.S. 898 (1997).

**Application:** Minnesota's lawsuit alleges federal operations have effectively commandeered local police resources, forcing them to respond to chaos created by federal raids, straining public safety budgets (over $2 million in overtime in days).

### 3.4 Fourteenth Amendment: Equal Protection and Due Process

**Racial Profiling Allegations:** The ACLU class-action lawsuit alleges systematic racial and ethnic profiling, particularly targeting Somali-American communities, in violation of the Equal Protection Clause.

**Evidentiary Context:** Presidential statements referring to Somali people as "garbage" and stating "we don't want them in our country" provide evidence of discriminatory animus.

## 5. LEGAL ANALYSIS: WAR POWERS AND CONGRESSIONAL AUTHORITY

### 5.1 Constitutional Framework

> "The Congress shall have Power... To declare War..."
> — U.S. Constitution, Article I, Section 8

The Constitution vests war-making authority in Congress, not the Executive.

### 5.2 War Powers Resolution (1973)

50 U.S.C. § 1541 et seq. requires:

1. **Consultation:** The President "in every possible instance shall consult with Congress before introducing United States Armed Forces into hostilities"
2. **Reporting:** Written report to Congress within 48 hours of deployment
3. **Termination:** Forces must be withdrawn within 60 days absent Congressional authorization

**Application to Venezuela:** The January 3, 2026 operation was conducted without prior Congressional consultation or authorization, in direct violation of the War Powers Resolution.

### 5.3 Circumvention of Legal Oversight

Reports indicate the Defense Secretary dismissed top Judge Advocate General (JAG) officers, sidelining the military's internal legal advisors who ensure compliance with the laws of war. This creates structural conditions for unlawful orders.

## 6. PTSD AND PUBLIC TRAUMA: LEGAL FRAMEWORK

### 6.1 DSM-5-TR Criterion A: Trauma Exposure Standard

The Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders (DSM-5-TR) defines PTSD Criterion A as:

> **Exposure to actual or threatened death, serious injury, or sexual violence** in one or more of the following ways:
> 1. Directly experiencing the traumatic event(s)
> 2. Witnessing, in person, the event(s) as it occurred to others
> 3. Learning that the traumatic event(s) occurred to a close family member or close friend
> 4. Experiencing repeated or extreme exposure to aversive details of the traumatic event(s)

**Critical Legal Point:** There is **no requirement** that the violence be foreign, wartime, or non-governmental. State violence, threats of force, and militarized enforcement qualify if exposure is real and repeated.
